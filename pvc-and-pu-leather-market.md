<p><p>PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) and PU (Polyurethane) leather are synthetic materials widely used as alternatives to genuine leather. PVC leather is made from a plastic base, known for its durability, easy maintenance, and cost-effectiveness. Key trends influencing the market include a rising preference for eco-friendly and sustainable materials, leading to innovations in manufacturing processes that reduce environmental impact. Additionally, advancements in technology are enhancing the quality and appeal of synthetic leathers, making them more competitive with genuine leather. As consumers become more conscious of sustainability, the demand for high-quality, durable, and aesthetically pleasing alternatives to leather is expected to further propel market growth, positioning PVC and PU leather as popular choices across various applications.</p></p>
