<p><ul><li>Stable Isotopes</li><li>Radioisotopes</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The medical isotopes market is primarily divided into two types: stable isotopes and radioisotopes. Stable isotopes, such as carbon-13 and nitrogen-15, are used in diagnostics and therapeutic applications without undergoing radioactive decay. Radioisotopes, like Technetium-99m and Iodine-131, emit radiation and are commonly used in imaging and cancer treatments. Each type serves a critical role in medical diagnostics and therapy, with stable isotopes focusing on non-radioactive applications and radioisotopes providing pivotal insights through their decay properties.</p></p>
