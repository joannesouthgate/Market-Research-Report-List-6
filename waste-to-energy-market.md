<p><p>The Waste-to-Energy market encompasses two primary technology types: thermal technologies and biochemical reactions. Thermal technologies, such as incineration and gasification, utilize high temperatures to convert waste into energy, producing electricity or heat. In contrast, biochemical reactions involve processes like anaerobic digestion, where microorganisms break down organic waste to generate biogas, which can be used for energy production. Both methods aim to reduce landfill waste while harnessing renewable energy, contributing to sustainable waste management and energy generation.</p></p>
