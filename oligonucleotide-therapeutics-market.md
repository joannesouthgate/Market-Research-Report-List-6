<p><p>The oligonucleotide therapeutics market primarily includes three types: Antisense Oligonucleotides (ASOs), Aptamers, and Other markets. ASOs are short strands of nucleic acids designed to bind to specific RNA molecules, modulating gene expression to treat various diseases. Aptamers are oligonucleotides that can bind to specific proteins or small molecules, functioning as targeted therapeutics or diagnostics. The "Other" category encompasses emerging oligonucleotide-based therapies, including RNA interference agents and CRISPR-related technologies, broadening treatment options for diverse medical conditions.</p></p>
