<p><p>The commercial vehicle propeller shaft market features two primary types: rigid shafts and hollow shafts. Rigid shafts are solid, providing strength and durability, making them suitable for heavy-duty applications. In contrast, hollow shafts are lighter and offer improved efficiency due to reduced weight, enhancing fuel economy. The choice between the two depends on factors such as vehicle design, weight distribution, and performance requirements. Both shaft types play crucial roles in transmitting power from the engine to the wheels effectively.</p></p>
